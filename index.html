Project Context

An automotive customization storefront for interior products requiring a shopping experience that could guide buyers through complex variant choices without overwhelming them.

The Problem

Automotive products with multiple customization options are difficult to present clearly. Buyers need to understand what they're getting before committing — a standard Shopify product page rarely handles this well.

My Role

Prototyped the concept in Lovable, then evolved it into a custom headless frontend using TanStack Start — with Shopify managing all product data, cart, and checkout logic.

What I Would Test Next

Whether a guided configuration step — showing buyers a live preview of their selection — reduces hesitation on complex variant combinations. Worth testing before assuming the current selection UI is optimal.

Observation 01

"Buyers should not need to scroll before they understand what the product is for. The value proposition belongs at the top — not buried in the description."

Observation 02

"The primary action should not compete with several equally weighted buttons. Wishlist, Share, and Compare are secondary actions — treating them the same as Add to Cart creates visual noise at the decision point."

Observation 03

"Trust information is most useful beside the decision it supports. Shipping and return details that appear below the fold, after the button, are less likely to reduce hesitation."

This is a demonstration of my review process. Actual recommendations depend on the store, products, customers, and available data — not a fixed list of rules.

with me looks like.

01 — PROJECT CLARITY
Define the scope first

We define the store, audience, scope, responsibilities, and desired outcome before any building starts. Unclear requirements lead to unclear storefronts.

Prepared for what comes next

The final implementation is checked, documented, and prepared for launch or team handoff. You leave with something you can actually manage.

No disappearing. No mystery about project status. You receive clear progress updates and practical next steps throughout — not silence followed by a surprise delivery.
