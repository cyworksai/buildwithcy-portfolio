Project Overview

What this project is about.

PawPops is a Shopify concept store for pet treats and wellness products — built to show how I approach a real product-based storefront from the ground up.

The goal was to avoid a basic template look and build something intentional — from the homepage layout and product messaging, to the badges, visuals, and app setup. Every section was built to help a customer understand or trust the product.

The challenge

Pet wellness is a crowded category. The store had to feel genuinely trustworthy and brand-considered — not another generic template with stock photos. Every design decision had to earn its place.

Visual Design & Product Assets

Warm, friendly, and built
for customer trust.

I created and prepared all the visual assets used throughout the store — product photos, mockups, website banners, badges, and promotional-style graphics.

Using Canva and Photoshop, I edited and styled the visuals to match the warm pet wellness brand direction and make the store feel polished, trustworthy, and ready to sell.

Design direction

Soft, playful, and approachable — without looking messy or overly childish. Rounded details, warm product visuals, clean sections, and trust-building graphics that make the store feel cute, clear, and credible.

Why It Works

Designed for the customer, not just the brand.

Instead of only making the site look nice, I focused on how customers would understand the product, trust the brand, and move toward buying.

  • Customers can quickly understand what the product is
  • The brand feels warm, approachable, and trustworthy
  • Clear product benefits and visual hierarchy guide the eye
  • Product visuals make the store feel polished and realistic
  • The layout works well on both desktop and mobile
  • Practical Shopify setup and app integrations included

What I would test next

Whether adding a short "Why PawPops?" section above the fold reduces bounce rate by helping first-time visitors understand the brand before they see products to buy.
